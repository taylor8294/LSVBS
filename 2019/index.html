The LifeSight ageOmeter

The LifeSight ageOmeter helps you explore the age at which you may be able to afford to retire – your predicted LifeSight Age. You can use the ageOmeter to play around with different scenarios to better understand the impact of changing your contributions or investment choices.

It is important to note that the ageOmeter is just a modelling tool based on certain assumptions, and so your LifeSight Age is just an estimate and not a guarantee of when you can retire. Itâs possible that the age you can afford to retire in practice will fall outside the range shown in the ageOmeter, and so you should think of the range shown as just a guide to help you plan. You are responsible for saving for and planning your retirement and we recommend you seek financial advice if you are not sure about your savings and investments in LifeSight.

You can read more about how your predicted LifeSight Age has been calculated and the assumptions that have been made in our FAQ document. Remember that the assumptions made in the ageOmeter may not be right for your own situation.

Note:

If youâve recently made a request to change your investments in LifeSight, then your LifeSight Age may not yet take this into account. You can still try out different scenarios and see potential LifeSight Ages, but you would be unable to then go on and apply any changes until your pending changes have been completed.

The Saving Planner

The Savings Planner helps give you a bigger picture view of your overall financial position, allowing you to explore your potential retirement income in more detail.

The Savings Planner has been designed to work best on a desktop computer. If you are using a mobile device or tablet then you may want to revisit once you are able to access a desktop.

It is important to note that the Savings Planner is just a modelling tool, based on a series of assumptions. Investment returns are uncertain, and so the projections shown are just a guide to help you plan and not a guarantee of how long your savings will last. You are responsible for managing your savings and we recommend you seek financial advice if you are not sure about your investment choices or your retirement options.

You can read more about how to use the Savings Planner and the assumptions that have been made in our FAQ document. Remember that the assumptions made in the Savings Planner may not be right for your own situation.

Note:

If you’ve recently made a request to change your investments in LifeSight, then please note that the Savings Planner may not yet take this into account.

The Spending Planner

The Spending Planner helps give you a wider picture of your retirement spending by letting you explore how your LifeSight savings and other sources of retirement savings and investments can be combined to help fund your retirement.

The Spending Planner has been designed to work best on a desktop computer. If you are using a mobile device or tablet then you may want to revisit once you are able to access a desktop.

It is important to note that the Spending Planner is just a modelling tool, based on a series of assumptions. Investment returns are uncertain, and so the projections shown are just a guide to help you plan and not a guarantee of how long your savings will last. You are responsible for managing your savings in drawdown and we recommend you seek financial advice if you are not sure about your investment choices or your withdrawals.

You can read more about how to use the Spending Planner and the assumptions that have been made in our FAQ document. Remember that the assumptions made in the Spending Planner may not be right for your own situation.

Note:

If you’ve recently made a request to change your investments in LifeSight, then please note that the Spending Planner may not yet take this into account.
